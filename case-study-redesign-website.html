Care Rescue LA

Role: UX Research

Timeframe: Two Weeks

01 Research Goal

CARE Rescue LA is a non-profit organization saving animals in Southern California and surrounding areas.

Key Research questions and areas:
  • Is a radical website redesign justified over incremental changes?
  • At what part of the adoption/foster process is the website coming to place.
  • Identify needs and opportunities and to improve the usability of the website.
  • Observe and understand users current adoption process.

02 Methodology

2-week field study to assess the effectiveness of CARE Rescue LA’s website as participants used it . Study participants were located in Los Angeles, CA.

Phase 01: Conduct user surveys for the target demographic.
Phase 02: Competitive usability testing with CARE's site and website of competitor.

03 Industry Analysis

Competitive Analysis of companies in the same industry sector or market niche located in Los Angeles & surroundings.

04 Qualitative Survey

The participants of the survey were former adopters of CARE as well as fosters. The goal of it was to find out at what part of the adoption/foster process the website comes to use — discovering pain points and opportunities for the site of Care Rescue LA.

07 Competitive Usability Testing

To understand the strengths and weaknesses of the current design, I conducted remote usability tests. We did also test the website of a competitor to garner ideas and gain a more in-depth insight into what design elements work or fail.

Pain points that arose from the usability testing:
  • The missing pet profiles of the adoptable animals was the biggest concern and caused confusion of the participants.
  • Participants didn't understand why they get sent to Instagram after clicking on a picture.
  • No status of the animals if they are still available
  • Inability to apply for the adoptable pet through their pet profile
  • No cohesivness with animal images - some images are labeled with the name of the animal others aren't
Positive thoughts that came up in the usability testing:
  • Seeing pictures of the team makes the organization feel very approachable and personable
  • The checklist and application form is straightforward and easy to understand
  • Color scheme is calming and trustworthy

09 Overall Themes and Opportunities

01
The research showed that a radical website redesign wouldn't be justified. The discovered pain points can be fixed with incremental changes, and there isn't enough reason for a complete overhaul.

02
The #1 suggestion I received was about the missing pet profiles and the adoptable pets page. The interested adopters would love to see what animals are adoptable and have descriptions about each pet so they can get to know their personality.

03
The primary use of the website is to fill out the foster and adoption application. We should move that feature to a more prominent location of the site so the user, when going to the website, immediately sees it.

04
The users care for the rescue animals and would love to read about the rescue animals' happy ending. At the moment, one cat's story (the adorable Marty) got told on the page Furever Homes, and it would be a great opportunity if more and more post-adoption stories would get added to it.

05
Currently, users are getting sent back and forth between the organization's Instagram channel and the website, and the goal should be to keep the users on the site.

10 Conclusion

I loved doing this project, especially since I was able to help a great organization that puts all their energy into saving animals. What I wished I would have done differently is the user survey. I was so focused on finding out through the survey how the users are using the site that I didn’t ask important questions that I needed for the user persona.

Something I’m proud of is the competitive usability testing and journey map. I got valuable feedback and thoughts from the participants that gave me new insights about how they see the website.

I learned so much through this project, and I’m looking forward to using my newly gained insights on a future project.

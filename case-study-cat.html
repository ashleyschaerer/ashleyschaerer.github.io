More Than Fluffy

Find your match.

Role: Research, UI/UX, Visual

Tool: Sketch/Figma, inVision

VIEW PROTOTYPE

01 Intro

This project started as a pet adoption application, but through early research, I identified that what the market really needed/wanted was an adoption app that was solely about cats.

02 Problem

Currently, 3.2 million cats are waiting at shelters or foster families for their forever home, and the reality is that young and cute kittens get adopted fastest while older and less photogenic cats have to wait a long time for their forever home.

03 Solution

Creating a cat adoption app that matches users with cats based on personality and lifestyle and to give less photogenic and older cats a chance in finding a home.

04 Process

The path to Healthvitae consisted of a detailed and careful study of user research, competitive analysis, user personas, user stories/flows, wireframes, mockups, branding, prototypes and user testing - all with various iterations:

User Research Highlights

I wanted to find out if there is even a need for a medical records storage application. Is there a desire to have easy access to their medical data, or do people think it doesn't matter? I as well wanted to find out which age groups would be interested in a health application.

Competitive Analysis

My competitors went a similar route as I, and I realized to stand out it’s necessary to go a different path then they. All of my competitors either did a dog & cat app or an only dog adoption app. None of them prioritized cats, so I found a gap for myself by doing a cat adoption app based on the user's personality and lifestyle.

Practice Fusion is subscription based and cheaper than its competitors. The weakness is the conservative interface and that there is no mobile version available, which gives them opportunities if they would re-design the interface and as well create a mobile app.





Google Drive is enabling with Google Slides, Google Docs, and Google Sheets, and an easy way to collaborate. One of the threats is that if competitors find a way to differentiating its storage solution more or match Google's level of engagement with its customers, then it could be a serious threat to Google Drive.





DropBox strength is that it has a very user-friendly interface. One of the weaknesses is that they offer less free storage space compared to Google Drive and the security and privacy issues.

Personas

Based on the survey and research I did prior I identified two target audiences: Millennials and Gen X.

User Stories

To establish necessary features, I created a list of user stories based on potential user tasks. The user stories were refined and prioritized to define a minimum viable product.



As a new user:
  • I want to sign up for an account
  • I want to take a quiz to find a cat which fits to me
  • I want to see a selection of adoptable pets
As a returning user:
  • I want to go to my saved list of adoptable cats
  • I want to receive notifications about new matches
  • I want to read about the cat's personality traits and quirks.
VIEW USER STORIES

Sitemap and User Flows

Initially, I was planning on creating a quiz, so the user can take it, and based on its answers, he will get a selection of adoptable pets. And besides that, he would as well go through an onboarding experience (asking for location and notifications). But after some additional thinking, I decided not to have a separate quiz and instead to put it into the onboarding process. The reason for it is that I don’t have enough questions to justify having the user taking a quiz AND going through the onboarding as well. Those are too many steps, and I would risk losing users through that.

User Testing

For the testing I did onsite as well as remote testing. I created the prototype in inVision and unfortunately it had some flaws. Those caused some distraction for the participants. I got feedback but mainly for the flaws in the prototype instead about the wireframes.

  • "Get Started" button in hero header needs to be more prominent since it got overlooked by both participants

  • "Upload" and "Add" button need get have more importance than the other buttons next to it

Concept and Branding

The name of the app is bold and flashy, and that’s why it also needs a vibrant and flashy color to support the feeling and message of the app.

Hot Pink Color Meaning
The color pink is the color of universal love of oneself and of others. Pink represents friendship, affection, harmony, inner peace, and approachability.

Usability Testing

The second round of user testing worked much better, and I got some helpful feedback about the prototype this time. Overall all participants agreed that it looks like a health application, the colors are suited well for it. The things I should improve were:



  • Green font is too bright and hard to read
  • Overall font size is on the smaller side
  • Logo is too small
  • Some buttons had outlines and some not.

05 Conclusion

Healthvitae achieves its goal to give users fast and easy access to their medical records. Over the course of many testing sessions and design iterations, we developed a user interface that is simplistic yet modern and easy to operate. While I'm pleased with the final solution, I wish my early testing phase would have worked out better. But running into those issues also taught me to do not the same mistakes next time and I also learned the big importance of user testing and what impact that has on your product.

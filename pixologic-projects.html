Healthvitae

Healthvitae allows individuals to store their medical records.

Role: Research, UI/UX, Visual

Tool: Sketch/Figma, inVision

VIEW PROTOTYPE

01 Intro

With all the challenges people face, having access to their medical records shouldn't be one of them. After researching about our current healthcare system, I established issues I would set out to fix, project goals, and who my users were.

02 Problem

In a time where more and more young people move away for a job opportunity, not having possession over your medical data is not just annoying; it is also dangerous. Once users leave their healthcare provider, they will lose the collected data about themselves because it will stay with their former provider, instead of going with them. Moreover, what happens then is that the continuity of medical care is at risk when users cannot provide a complete medical history to health professionals they see for the first time.

03 Solution

Allow users to aggregate their medical records and have easy access to all their health information without having to go through your current healthcare provider.

04 Process

The path to Healthvitae consisted of a detailed and careful study of user research, competitive analysis, user personas, user stories/flows, wireframes, mockups, branding, prototypes and user testing - all with various iterations:

Preference Testing

Usability Testing

The second round of user testing worked much better, and I got some helpful feedback about the prototype this time. Overall all participants agreed that it looks like a health application, the colors are suited well for it. The things I should improve were:



  • Green font is too bright and hard to read
  • Overall font size is on the smaller side
  • Logo is too small
  • Some buttons had outlines and some not.

05 Conclusion

Healthvitae achieves its goal to give users fast and easy access to their medical records. Over the course of many testing sessions and design iterations, we developed a user interface that is simplistic yet modern and easy to operate. While I'm pleased with the final solution, I wish my early testing phase would have worked out better. But running into those issues also taught me to do not the same mistakes next time and I also learned the big importance of user testing and what impact that has on your product.

Healthvitae

Healthvitae allows individuals to store their medical records.

Role: Research, UI/UX, Visual

Tool: Sketch/Figma, inVision

VIEW PROTOTYPE

01 Intro

With all the challenges people face, having access to their medical records shouldn't be one of them. After researching about our current healthcare system, I established issues I would set out to fix, project goals, and who my users were.

02 Problem

In a time where more and more young people move away for a job opportunity, not having possession over your medical data is not just annoying; it is also dangerous. Once users leave their healthcare provider, they will lose the collected data about themselves because it will stay with their former provider, instead of going with them. Moreover, what happens then is that the continuity of medical care is at risk when users cannot provide a complete medical history to health professionals they see for the first time.

03 Solution

Allow users to aggregate their medical records and have easy access to all their health information without having to go through your current healthcare provider.

04 Design Process

The path to Healthvitae consisted of a detailed and careful study of user research, competitive analysis, user personas, user stories/flows, wireframes, mockups, branding, prototypes and user testing - all with various iterations:

Competitive Analysis

For my medical record cloud storage idea, I looked into three different cloud storage for three specific reasons.

Practice Fusion because it is the most popular medical record application in the market, Google Drive because mainly individuals are using it, and my application has the same target audience. Also, lastly DropBox because of its user-friendly interface.

Practice Fusion is subscription based and cheaper than its competitors. The weakness is the conservative interface and that there is no mobile version available, which gives them opportunities if they would re-design the interface and as well create a mobile app.

Google Drive is enabling with Google Slides, Google Docs, and Google Sheets, and an easy way to collaborate. One of the threats is that if competitors find a way to differentiating its storage solution more or match Google's level of engagement with its customers, then it could be a serious threat to Google Drive.

DropBox strength is that it has a very user-friendly interface. One of the weaknesses is that they offer less free storage space compared to Google Drive and the security and privacy issues.

Personas

Based on the survey and research I did prior I identified two target audiences: Millennials and Gen X.

Millenial

Michael Thompson

26 yrs old and Graphic Designer

Frustrations:

  • Michael moved to Germany, and because of that he lost all of his medical data since it stayed with his former healthcare provider

  • Everything which has to do with doctors and hospital seems to be a complicated and confusing process

Goals:

  • Having all of his medical histories electronically accessible

  • He is not a fan of bureaucracy and hassle, he wants to be able to access his medical information easy and quick

Gen X

Sarah Abott

45 yrs old and mother of two

Frustrations:

  • All of her family's medical records are scattered between different doctors and different practices.

  • She always has to worry about her dad's health problems.

Goals:

  • Able to manage all of her family's medical records in one single account.

  • Helping her father to keep track of his medications using cloud storage.

User Stories

To establish necessary features, I created a list of user stories based on potential user tasks. The user stories were refined and prioritized to define a minimum viable product.



As a new user:
  • I want to create an account
  • I want to know how much it costs
  • I want to upload my medical records
As a returning user:
  • I want to sign in
  • I want to see my medical history
  • I want to share my data with my doctor

User Testing

For the testing, I did onsite as well as remote testing. I created the prototype in inVision, and unfortunately, it had some flaws. Those caused some distraction for the participants. I got feedback but mainly for the flaws in the prototype instead about the wireframes.

  • "Get Started" button in hero header needs to be more prominent since it got overlooked by both participants

  • "Upload" and "Add" button need get have more importance than the other buttons next to it

Concept and Branding

The goal was that everybody who sees the logo immediately knows that it's about health. Because of that I chose a heart with a pulse. It's a minimalistic icon but highly effective. It also represents what the brand stands for: Modern and friendly.

Usability Testing

The second round of user testing worked much better, and I got some helpful feedback about the prototype this time. Overall all participants agreed that it looks like a health application, the colors are suited well for it. The things I should improve were:

  • Green font is too bright and hard to read
  • Overall font size is on the smaller side
  • Logo is too small
  • Some buttons had outlines and some not.

05 Conclusion

Healthvitae achieves its goal to give users fast and easy access to their medical records. Over the course of many testing sessions and design iterations, we developed a user interface that is simplistic yet modern and easy to operate. While I'm pleased with the final solution, I wish my early testing phase would have worked out better. But running into those issues also taught me to do not the same mistakes next time and I also learned the big importance of user testing and what impact that has on your product.
